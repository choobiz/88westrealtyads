# Foreclosure / Power-of-Sale / Distressed Property Buyer Landing Pages — Competitor Research

**Project:** 88 West Realty `go.88westrealty.com` Foreclosure Campaign
**Author:** Research agent for Amir Omidvar
**Date:** 2026-04-20
**Audience:** Greater Vancouver foreclosure buyers — both investors with cash AND owner-occupiers/first-time buyers chasing bargains
**Lead magnet hook:** "Free Foreclosure List Updated Daily"

---

## TL;DR — Top 7 Findings

1. **Almost no competitor in Greater Vancouver runs a real conversion-optimized landing page.** Vancouver "foreclosure" pages are universally ugly IDX result-list dumps on `myrealpage.com` themes (Macnabs, Simon Clayton, Stan Stanchev, BC Prime, Reimers, Sutton/Condo Presale Direct). The headline is usually just a page title like "Vancouver Foreclosures" with a generic "set alerts / sign up" CTA. **This is a wide-open positioning gap.**
2. **Toronto is 5+ years ahead.** `powerofsaleplus.ca` (RE/MAX Plus City Team) and `powerofsalelistings.com` (Royal LePage agent Jonathan Alphonso) are the model: branded-domain LPs, daily-updated databases, "Get Power of Sale Alert" CTAs, FAQ sections, YouTube explainer reels, and multi-source aggregator framing ("we aggregate from 3 active data sources").
3. **The dominant lead magnet pattern is "free account + daily email alerts + saved search"** — NOT a downloadable PDF. Almost every competitor delivers a gated MLS-feed-style portal where you create an account, pick filters, and receive new listings via email. Form fields are typically minimal: email + name + phone + optional message. A few (Sutton/Condo Presale Direct, Realtyvibe, Stan Stanchev) require area selection.
4. **Trust is built almost exclusively via three signals**: (a) **brokerage logo** (Royal LePage, Macdonald Realty, RE/MAX, Sutton, Oakwyn), (b) **realtor face photo + license + phone**, and (c) **track-record numbers** ("$50M in loan assets recovered" — Simon Clayton; "Since 2009, no Realtor in Vancouver has listed more homes by Court order"). BBB, Google reviews, and "years experience" badges are surprisingly rare in Canada (more common in Phoenix/US aggregators).
5. **The clearest "steal-able" hooks are scarcity + speed**, not discount. "Find Power of Sale Properties Before Anyone Else" (powerofsalelistings.com), "Get the best real estate deals first, before they hit the mass market!" (foreclosure.com), "Updated Daily 24/7" (jerrycharlton.com Calgary). Almost no Canadian competitor uses explicit % discount language ("Save 20-40%") because it's harder to defend in BC's competitive court-bid environment.
6. **Offer stacking is virtually absent in Canada — pure white space.** Stan Stanchev's "Buyers FREE Real Estate Services" page is the only real example: free guide on buying foreclosures + 1% savings guarantee (10% commission rebate if not met) + free 1-year resale guarantee. Toronto and US competitors don't bundle education + comps + financing intro at all. **This is a positioning weapon.**
7. **The main anti-pattern to avoid is the lender-pitch positioning trap.** Two of the top-ranked Vancouver "court-ordered sale" sites (`bcprimerealty.com`, `simonclayton.ca`) write ALL their copy for *banks and lawyers seeking listing agents*, not for *buyers*. They confuse the buyer who lands there from an ad and bury the buyer signup. 88West can win by being the first true *buyer-side* foreclosure brand in Greater Vancouver.

## 5. Lead-Magnet Mechanics — How They Actually Work

Across 25+ sites studied, the patterns cluster into 5 archetypes:

### Archetype A — "Free Account + Daily Email Alerts" (most common, ~60%)
- **Form:** Email + Name + Phone + (optional) area selector
- **Delivery:** User creates account → IDX-style portal → saved-search results emailed daily/weekly
- **Examples:** Rossetti, Sutton/Condo Presale Direct, powerofsalelistings.com, jerrycharlton.com, realtyvibe.ca
- **Pros:** Recurring email touchpoints, segmentation by area, retention via "saved searches"
- **Cons:** High friction (account creation), commodity offer (every realtor offers this)

### Archetype B — "Get the List Now via Email" (~20%)
- **Form:** Email + Name + (sometimes) Phone, single message field
- **Delivery:** One immediate email with a CSV/PDF/list, then ongoing nurture
- **Examples:** Reimers ("emailed monthly"), Stan Stanchev VIP Insiders, foreclosure.com email alerts
- **Pros:** Low friction, instant gratification
- **Cons:** Hard to maintain freshness (which is why most evolved into Archetype A)

### Archetype C — "Subscription / Free Trial" (~10%, US-skewed)
- **Form:** Email + Credit Card (free trial then paid)
- **Delivery:** Full database/portal access
- **Examples:** Foreclosure.com, RealtyTrac, ForeclosureRadar (5-day free trial), Auction.com (free account + paid services)
- **Pros:** Qualifies serious buyers, monetizes data
- **Cons:** Friction, harder for realtors to use as pure lead magnet

### Archetype D — "Free Resource + Realtor Pitch" (~5%, but high quality)
- **Form:** Email + Name + Phone + needs assessment
- **Delivery:** Educational PDF or guide, then a 1:1 consultation booking
- **Examples:** Stan Stanchev "complete guide 'The process of buying foreclosure properties'", Reimers educational guide, BC Prime "30-min coffee" Calendly
- **Pros:** Pre-qualifies buyer intent, builds authority
- **Cons:** Requires content production

### Archetype E — "Public List + Soft Pitch" (rare, anti-conversion)
- **Form:** None for the main list; secondary form for valuation/upgrade
- **Delivery:** Public IDX page; trust play
- **Examples:** A-Team Calgary, themacnabs.com
- **Pros:** Strong SEO and trust signal
- **Cons:** No lead capture (kills retargeting)

### Recommended for 88West: **Hybrid A + B + D**
- Hero CTA: "Free Foreclosure List — Updated Daily" → email-only short form (email + name + phone + optional postal/area)
- Delivery: Welcome email with CURRENT list (PDF or web link to live filtered MLS feed) + daily auto-email of new POS / court-order listings + 3-day educational drip ("How BC court-ordered sales actually work", "Schedule A explained", "Vancouver vs Surrey foreclosure pricing")
- Secondary CTA on long page: Calendly "30-min foreclosure strategy call" for high-intent users

## 7. Trust Signal Strategies — How Competitors De-Sketch the Foreclosure Vibe

Foreclosure marketing has an inherent "predator" risk — competitors actively work to deflect it. Patterns observed:

### 7.1 Brokerage logo + face photo (universal)
- Every credible site shows the brokerage badge prominently (Royal LePage, RE/MAX, Macdonald, Sutton, Oakwyn, Macnabs are the BC players). Realtor face photos near the form de-anonymize the offer. **`bcprimerealty.com` even sticks a Calendly "30-min coffee" CTA right next to the agent photo — disarming.**

### 7.2 Track-record numbers
- *Simon Clayton:* "$50,000,000 in loan assets recovered" + "Since 2009, no Realtor in Vancouver has listed more homes by Court order than I have."

### 7.3 Educational FAQ / process explainer
- Almost universal in Toronto and Calgary; weakly developed in Vancouver. Topics: *What is POS / Power of Sale vs Foreclosure / As-is risk / Schedule B / Financing / Hidden liens / Closing timelines*. Doubles as SEO content and trust play.
- powerofsaleplus.ca's FAQ is the cleanest version (6 questions, 50-100 words each).

### 7.4 Risk disclosure as service offer (the "WARNING" pattern)
- Stan Stanchev's WARNING-style top paragraph flips title/lien/grow-op/asbestos risks into reasons to hire him. *"We will research and provide…This can save you $$$-THOUSANDS-$$$"* — flips a perceived danger into a perceived savings. Steal this exact structure.

### 7.5 Video / podcast / YouTube embeds
- powerofsaleplus.ca embeds 3 YouTube Shorts. Power of Sale Listings doesn't, but RE/MAX Plus City has a YouTube channel.
- Auction.com has 5 video testimonials + 3 educational videos.
- **No Vancouver foreclosure LP has video.** Massive opportunity.

### 7.6 BBB / accreditation / regulator badges
- US: Auction.com, Foreclosure.com, ROI Properties all show industry trust badges. BBB accreditation common.
- Canada: Most competitors lean on RECBC / RECA license + brokerage rather than BBB. Add MLS Reciprocity logo + agent photo + license number.

### 7.7 Social proof (specifically on foreclosure)

## 12. Three to Five Anti-Patterns to Avoid

1. **The lender-pitch confusion (BC Prime, Simon Clayton)** — writing "we represent banks and lawyers" copy on a buyer-traffic page. Pick one audience per page. 88West's go-landing should be 100% buyer-focused.
2. **The fragmented region-link IDX dump (Stan Stanchev)** — 16 separate "click here for Burnaby condos / Tri-Cities houses / Whistler condos…" links above the form. Buyers bounce. Use one unified search filter, not 16 region tokens.
3. **The "We don't gate, we're transparent" trap (A-Team Calgary)** — exposing the full list with no email gate gives away your retargeting list. The right play is a low-friction email gate ("see 2 free, then enter email for full 47-listing PDF"), NOT pure publicness.
4. **The empty-inventory page (Bernie Doyle: *"There are currently no listings on this page, please check back shortly."*)** — kills credibility. Always show 6+ listings; if no real foreclosures are active, show recent SOLD foreclosures with price-vs-list comparisons.
5. **The "discount %" claim that triggers RECBC scrutiny** — promising "Save 20-40% off market" in BC is regulatorily risky and competitively undefendable. Instead use *"often priced below comparable market value"* (powerofsalelistings.com phrasing — vague enough to be defensible, strong enough to convert). Also avoid the predatory framing of `myleaddog.com`-style mailing-list operations that scrape filings and aim at distressed sellers — 88West is buyer-side and doesn't go near pre-foreclosure homeowner outreach.
