## TL;DR

- **The seed term "vancouver foreclosures" is the demand spine.** Every credible BC competitor ranks for it and its city/neighbourhood variants. Estimated 800–2,400 monthly searches (broad concept) across BC; Greater Vancouver geo-targeted slice is the highest-CPC segment (~$3.50–$8.00 CPC range based on real-estate vertical benchmarks).
- **"Court-ordered sale" + "judicial sale" are the BC-specific intent keywords.** They beat "foreclosure" for true buyer intent in BC — but lower volume (~50–200/mo each). Bid them at higher match-type precision because they're cheaper to win.
- **The "process anxiety" cluster is hugely under-served by paid search.** Owner-occupiers Google "is buying a foreclosure safe", "how does foreclosure work in BC", "first time buyer foreclosure" — these are mid-funnel queries that Stan Stanchev / Reimers / Pazder rank for organically but virtually NO ONE bids on. Soft, cheap, defensible.
- **City-specific modifiers (north vancouver / burnaby / richmond) double the head-term coverage.** Each city term has its own search slice; aggregator (`realestatecoalharbour.com`) ranks for all of them with separate URLs. We can run one ad group per city for tighter quality score.
- **Negative-keyword discipline is critical.** Seller-side phrases ("stop foreclosure", "save my home", "facing foreclosure") will pull predator-style traffic that doesn't convert; "ontario", "toronto", "calgary", "alberta", "USA", "florida" will pull cross-border traffic with zero intent for BC court-ordered listings.
- **Organic SERP is dominated by IDX dumps + brokerage pages.** Winning paid is faster than winning organic — Reimers, Macnabs, BCHB, Sam Huang's `realestatecoalharbour.com`, Simon Clayton, KeyHomes occupy positions 1–10 for most head terms. Long-tail and process-anxiety terms have clearer organic openings.
- **Realistic CPL at $10/day is $25–$60.** Roughly 1–2 leads/day after warmup; needs careful negative list and tight match types.

### $10/day budget realistic outcomes

| Metric | Realistic range |
|---|---|
| Daily clicks | 2–6 |
| Daily impressions | 40–150 |
| Avg CPC | $2.50–$5.00 |
| Conversion rate (lead form fill) | 6–12% |
| Daily leads | 0.5–1.5 |
| **CPL (cost per lead)** | **$25–$60** |
| Monthly leads (30 days) | 15–45 |

### CPL viability check

The Hormozi Money Model has $99/mo continuity ("Off-Market Alpha") + variable upsell to commission deals. If only **5%** of leads convert to a buyer-agency engagement (industry-typical for cold paid traffic), and average commission is ~$15–25K per closed deal, **CPL up to $200 is profitable** on a 12–18 month attribution window. CPL of $25–$60 is comfortably in the green zone.

### Bid strategy
- **First 14 days:** Manual CPC, conservative bids ($1.50–$3.00 starter), Phrase + Exact match only. No Broad. No Smart Bidding (insufficient signal).
- **Days 15–30:** Switch to Maximize Conversions once 15+ conversions logged. Add Broad-Modified for AG3 + AG4 (process-anxiety long-tails).
- **Day 30+:** Layer Target CPA at $40–50 once stable. Add Performance Max only after 100+ form-fill conversions in account history.

### Match-type discipline
- AG1 (general) — Phrase only (high broad-match waste risk)
- AG2 (court-ordered) — Phrase + Exact (cheap + high-intent)
- AG3 (investor) — Phrase + Broad-Modified (allows discovery)
- AG4 (process anxiety) — Broad-Modified (educational long-tails benefit from variant matching)
- AG5 (city) — Phrase only
